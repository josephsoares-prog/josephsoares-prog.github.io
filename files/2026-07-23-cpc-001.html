The Succession Machine

Who actually runs Conservative politics now — the operative inventory.

The top line

Fourteen months after the 2025 election, Conservative power runs through two structures that no longer fully trust each other. Inside: an Opposition Leader's Office consolidated under Steve Outhouse, who since May 2026 holds both the chief of staff role and the national campaign manager title — parliamentary office and campaign machine under one operative [CBC]. Outside: the Ontario/Harper-era operative class — Kory Teneycke, Nick Kouvalis, and the network around them — whose criticism of the 2025 campaign run by Jenni Byrne went public and stayed there.

The fault line stopped being deniable at the Calgary Stampede in July, when the leader's jab at "Liberal lobbyists from out east" — read universally as aimed at Teneycke — drew a public rebuke from the BC Conservative candidate Teneycke and Kouvalis were backing [National Observer].
