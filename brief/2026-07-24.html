OIL SHOCK

Oil tops $100 for the first time since May after Houthi attacks on Saudi tankers in the Red Sea

The National · thenationalnews.com ↗

Brent jumped 6.45% to $101.10 and WTI to $91.49 after the Encelia was set ablaze in the Red Sea, taking crude up more than 30% for July. This is no longer a headline spike — it is a second chokepoint pricing itself in, and Goldman is openly modelling $120 by Q4. Every budget, margin and inflation forecast built on cheaper energy is now obsolete; rebuild them at $100-plus.

Yahoo Finance · finance.yahoo.com ↗

Treat landed costs as structurally higher and reprice contracts now, not at renewal.
