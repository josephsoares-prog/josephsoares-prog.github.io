01
HORMUZ

US strikes Iran again as a tanker is hit in Hormuz; a 10-day ceasefire moves into focus

CNBC · cnbc.com ↗

The signal that matters is the split screen: Washington keeps striking while floating a 10-day pause, and a fresh tanker hit shows Iran can still bleed the strait. Read any ceasefire as a fragile breather to reposition, not an off-ramp — the risk premium in oil and shipping stays until Iran's anti-ship capability is actually degraded.

02
TARIFFS

Trump hits Canada with a 50% tariff under Section 338 — even on CUSMA-compliant goods

CBC News · cbc.ca ↗

Reaching for Section 338 — a dormant 1930 statute — to override CUSMA protections is the escalation: it tells every Canadian exporter that treaty coverage is no longer a shield. Autos, alcohol and dairy take the hit while energy, potash and critical minerals are spared, and the 30-day runway is the negotiating window. Model your supply chain for the tariff landing, not for a last-minute reprieve.

03
ENERGY

Yemen's Houthis declare a naval blockade of Saudi Arabia, widening the threat to oil supply

Al Jazeera · aljazeera.com ↗

With Hormuz already choked, a Houthi embargo puts Saudi Arabia's Red Sea escape valve at Bab al-Mandeb — roughly 7.4 million barrels a day in June — under fire too. If both chokepoints seize, up to a quarter of world oil and gas transit is exposed. This is the scenario that turns a Gulf war into a global supply shock; hedge energy exposure accordingly.

04
OIL

Saudi oil loadings fall 36% as Houthi threats disrupt the Bab al-Mandeb passage

The National · thenationalnews.com ↗

Loadings dropping from 9.5 to 6.1 million barrels a day is not a threat on paper — it is barrels already off the water. With over 70% of Saudi crude now routed via the Red Sea since Hormuz closed, the kingdom's diversification is being neutralized in real time. Expect physical tightness to feed the paper price faster than the market is pricing.

CANADA

Forbes · forbes.com ↗

Carney is betting that restraint plus a cost-to-American-consumers message beats a tit-for-tat spiral — a calculated split from Doug Ford's dollar-for-dollar demand. The read for business: Ottawa wants a deal inside the 30-day window and won't spook it with counter-tariffs first. Watch whether provincial hawks force his hand before the clock runs out.

The Street · thestreet.com ↗

With Brent parked in the high $80s, the market has stopped treating the Gulf as a spike and started treating it as a regime.
